During the Ukraine Recovery Conference in Berlin earlier this month, the interlinkage between military defence and economic recovery was emphasised. No investments and recovery projects can be safe and durable without sufficient air defence. At the same time, Ukraine needs swift economic recovery to sustain resilience in the face of Russian aggression.

In this session, Former Dutch Ambassador to Ukraine Robert Serry (Open Door Ukraine) and Julia Soldatiuk-Westerveld, Research Fellow EU & Global Affairs (Clingendael Institute for International Relations), will reflect on the key takeaways from the URC 2024, the priorities for recovery in the coming time, and talk about the early recovery projects that are already realised in Ukraine. Robert Serry will present his recovery project in Ukraine. Are you curious to know more about how to become active in Ukraine’s recovery and how to increase the impact of your recovery efforts? Join us in this session!

Russia and China have been steadily growing closer to each other in recent years. Mere weeks before Russia’s February 2022 invasion of Ukraine, Moscow and Beijing announced a strategic partnership with "no limits." The war and Western sanctions against Russia have significantly accelerated cooperation and trade between the two non-Western great powers, while also highlighting the substantial asymmetry between them. This workshop will discuss various dimensions of this growing partnership – geopolitics, economic relations, and military-technical cooperation – in order to get a sense of the nature and scope of this crucial geopolitical relationship that is reshaping the global order.

Over REKA

REKA is een initiatief dat tot doel heeft de aanwezige kennis in Nederland over Rusland en Oost-Europa uit te bouwen, publiek inzichtelijk te maken en beschikbaar te stellen voor de Nederlandse overheid.

'REKA' - Russisch voor rivier - slaat dan ook op de stroom van kennis die dit netwerk beoogt op gang te brengen. Daarvoor hoopt REKA een intensieve samenwerking op te zetten met kennisinstituten, media en andere organisaties binnen Nederland die zich met de regio Rusland en Oost-Europa bezighouden.

Het kennisnetwerk wordt gefinancierd door het Nederlands Ministerie van Buitenlandse Zaken. Het secretariaat wordt gevoerd door een consortium bestaande uit Instituut Clingendael en Raam op Rusland, Oekraïne, en Belarus, gevormd door Bob Deen, Niels Drost en Hubert Smeets.
